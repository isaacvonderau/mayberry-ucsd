Welcome to the Lab

Participate

Welcome to the Mayberry Laboratory for Multimodal Language Development. We investigate language acquisition and processing across the multiple sensory and motor modalities through which language occurs: sign language, spoken language, written language, and gesture. We use a variety of psycholinguistic and neurolinguistic paradigms to discover how the child's early language experience affects adult language ability across these modalities.

Funding

Our research is currently funded by a grant from the National Institute of Deafness and Other Communication Disorders, 1R01DC012797.

Past Funding

Our research has been continuously funded for the past 25 years by several agencies, institutions and foundations. At UCSD these include the NIH, the Kavli Foundation, the UCSD Senate Grants Program, the UCSD Chancellors Collaboratories Program, and the Visual Language and Visual Learning Center at Gallaudet University funded by the National Science Foundation. At McGill University our research was funded by the NIH, NSERC (Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada), SSHRC (Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada), the Center for Research on Language, Mind,and Brain, and the GW Stairs Memorial Fund.

Participate

We are always looking for volunteers to participate in our research studies. Currently we are looking for 3 groups of individuals:

  • Group 1: Deaf ASL natives
  • Group 2: Foreign Deaf ASL signers (moved to US after 8 years old)
  • Group 3: Fluent hearing ASL signers (minimum 5 years experience)

Participants also must be:

  • right handed
  • free of any permament metal in their body
  • between the ages 18-45
